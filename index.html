Mathing to the Round of 32

12 groups. Top two go through automatically. The best eight of the twelve third‑place teams take the rest of the Round of 32 spots. Pick a team to see exactly where it stands.

How the best‑third‑place rule works

With 48 teams in 12 groups of four, only the top two from each group go through on group position alone. To reach 32 teams, FIFA also takes the eight best third‑place finishers from across all 12 groups — so all twelve third‑place teams are pooled together and ranked head‑to‑head.

That cross‑group ranking goes, in order: points → goal difference → goals scored → fewest cards (team conduct) → FIFA World Ranking as a last resort. This tracker ranks primarily by points, then goal difference and goals scored — conduct and FIFA ranking are only used as the very final tiebreak, same as FIFA's own rule, but edge cases decided purely by cards are rare enough that this tool may occasionally differ from the official call by a place.

Six of the twelve groups (G, H, I, J, K, L) still have a final matchday game to play. This tracker simulates every win/draw/loss combination for those six games — 729 in total — to work out whether a team is mathematically through, mathematically out, or still has work to do.

One simplification: in Groups G, H, I and K the fourth‑place team is still mathematically able to finish third instead. This tool tracks the result for the team currently sitting third rather than modelling that internal swap, so in a small number of scenarios the actual third‑place finisher could be a different team than shown here.
